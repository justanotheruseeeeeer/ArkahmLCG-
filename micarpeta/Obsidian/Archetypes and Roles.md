## A comprehensive list and comparison

In Arkham we can find various individuals eager to investigate the paranormal activities surrounding them. Ussually, we can classify them into on of four types - like a new Myers Briggs test-.

- **Seeker** : Highly educated individuals. It's not their first time dealing with the paranormal, their intelligence serves the purpose of ``advancing`` the investigation and gathering ``clues``. Alhtough they have previously met the horrors, their physical status tend to be... perilous.. ahem. 
- **Guardian**: Strong investigators, with a penchant for ``protecting`` others and eliminating threats swiftly, all while providing various forms of ``support``. Guardians can defeat the lovecraftian monsters in record time, they have access to numerous weapons... you won't get a chance to take a breather, maybe that's what you like.
- **Mystic**: <u>Have you ever wanted to communicate with the monsters... </u>No??!! Really?! You know you are in a game and your concept of fun is ``gaslighting`` your fellow investigators, threats and the game mechanics themselves. Wanna ``mess`` with the tempo, threats, future, past, chaos, definitional reality (I'm serious) and endings... then congratulations this is your class. It's an extremely well balanced class, you will mostly go with the flow and dynamics of the game, until you have gatherred enough ressources, thereafter you will proceed to ``ignore`` texts, skill tests, modify them at will, add xp, add turns, take away turns, see the future, change the past, fix the present and so much more! It's also a flex class, so you can hit and investigate at the same time, the difference is your concept or ``fun`` and "suporting" teammates. A quick warning, they tend to be pretty frail.
- **Rogue**: If none of the classes above tickle your fancy then maybe you want to act as a solo player in your collaborative game. Rogues are quite a varied and synergistic class, they behave like ``hypercarries flex``. Equipped with a minor in ``ramp`` and a major in ``tax evasion``, this elusive creature can hoard huge amounts of ressources, actions, cards, movements, and aura. They can literally cash in everything. As it should, rogues are costly and ``AP hungry``, however give them a good start and some support and you will literally fly whilst your teammates walk.
- **Survivor**: At last, the class we would realistically be in this scenario. Survivors, as the name clearly and politely points out, ``survive``. You will find yourself repeating skill tests until you finally succeed, or just not revealling choas tokens. In a way they are similar to mystics, but their manipulation comes out of fear, lest they witness the great horror of the sea, whilst mystics are arrogant and reckless. Survivors will ``unfail`` failed skill tests, or give a ``loser reward``. It is unfortunately one of the most disliked classes, since it has no clear defined strenght but rather a ``resistance``. Newer updates have shined a spotlight on them, though earlier versions feel quite clunky.

Now, what kind of synergies or archetypes are built into the game to aid each class?

- ``+2``: One of the earlist example is the "succed by at least 2" synergy. It will double your results as long as you succedd skill test by at least 2. It is found in both the **seeker** and **rogue** classes abundantly.
- ``search/draw``: Unique to **seekers**, it becomes a ``connectivity`` engine. A way to guarantee that each teammate gets their plan set up appropriately.
- ``fail`` : A flexible archeype, mostly associated with **survivors** though available to everyone. If you fail, you actually ``succed`` at something. LWIF is the best example.
- ``Discards``: **Survivors** had a glowup. They can now retrieve cards they already played or play cards at any point from their discards. Unlike **seekers**, we can find greater reproducibility.
- ``Blessings``: Available to **survivors, guardians and mystics**. You get bonuses, unstructured bonuses. 
- ``seal``: **Mystics** can exile threats to skill tests pretty much permanently. Now you can give everyone a 15% boost to their  ``tests``. It's a costly archetype, but as we will see it lines up with the general gaslighting of mystics.
- ``reveal``: The antithesis of ``sealing``, you want to reveal every single token you can. ==Je mehr desto besser==. It's like DnD advantage.
- ``see future``: **Mystics** can pay a small intangible price with their soul to see what's in store. Namely with the chaos tokens (Premonition) but also with the encounter deck (Scrying).
- ``Deny/Ignore``: Though it doesn't exist as an actual archetype, most of a mystic's ammunition relies heavily on denying negative effects or capitalizing off of them. 
-  ``succeed by``: **Mystics** can see the future and thell you exactly how short you'll fall. Available to **rogues**.
- ``MOney MOney Money!!!!``: Of course there's a **rogue** robbing the bank. Pay for extra actions, get money for those extra actions, pay for every event and asset you can. There's never a limit. Synergises particularly well with certain **guardians**.
- ``AcP``: Common to **guardians** and **rogues**. Gift and capitalize off of extra actions. Particularly strong with ``clocks``, since they allow many extra actions and even repeating an entire phase.
- ``Evade``: Of course the **rogue** sneaks past the enemy and launches a sneak attack while somehow robbing them...
- ``Connecting location``: **Rogues** and **seekers** love to teleport around the map.
- ``Engaged with``: It rewards **guardians** for distracting enemies, the opposite of ``evade``.
- ``Fix=1``: Don't you desire never pulling a single token?! Just take some risks or costs and get the reward. A ``rainbow`` archetype.
- ``Events/Assets/Skills with reduced cost``: Mostly found in **seeker** and **rogue** setups. It allows for reduced costs and easy playing of said cards.
- ``Take/Heal``: Convert life into a currency of your choice, particularly ``dm``.

Some notes and precautions are deemed necessary:
- ``Seal and Reveal``: They will appear alongside each other in **mystics** decks if we are not careful. **Antisynergy**
- ``Take``: Really risky, only to be used complementary to another game plan.
