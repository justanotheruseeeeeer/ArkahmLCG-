## Definitions

It results easier to divide the concepts into a pair of dichotomies.

***Add*** may refer to:
- A permanent `advantage` against various *skill-tests
- A temporary ``assistance`` against various *skill-tests*
- An *immediate* addition to the current active test

***Red*** may refer to:
- A permanent `reduction` of difficulty against various *skill-tests*
- A temporary `relief/easing` against various *skill-tests*
- An *immediate* reduction to the current active test

Akin to Dnd, where rolling with advantage doesn't equal the enemy rolling with disadvantage -with the latter _`being mostly better`_- in Arkham there is a slight advantage when reducing the test's difficulty or shroud to 0, since only the autofail will result in a failure regardless of your configuration.

-------------

***Seal*** may refer to:
- A semi-permanent `exile` of chaos tokens from the *Chaos Bag* 
- A temporary `ostracism` of chaos tokens from the *Chaos Bag*
- The ``holding`` of a token for later use

***Blessed/Cursed*** refers to:
- A modification of the *Chaos Bag* wherein new tokens are added
- Said Cursed/Blessed Tokens provide a **+/-2** modifier to the skill-test
- After said Tokens are ``revealed`` they provide their respective modifier and reveal an additional token
- This algorithm repeats until the 'chosen' tokens behave normally

Both mechanics can become quite strategic with the correct deck and party configuration. In fact, it shouldn't be a surprise to realize their combined synergy, though they are strong enough on their own.

I feel as though these mechanics are particularly powerful because they are oblivious to their own saturation, for the most part. Unlike a wide range of mechanics it seems to me these pair is immune to **Diminishing Returns**. Though they do behave similarly to the typical `def ignore` of certain videogames, with an explicit cap.
